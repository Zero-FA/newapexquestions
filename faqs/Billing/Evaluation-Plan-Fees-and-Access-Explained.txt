Evaluation Plan Fees and Access Explained

Overview
When you purchase an Evaluation, you pay a one-time fee for that specific account size and type.

The Evaluation you select at checkout is the one you will receive. The fee cannot be refunded, transferred, or changed to a different account size or type after purchase.

The fee is not a subscription and does not renew. Your purchase provides access to trade that Evaluation for 30 calendar days.


Purchasing an Evaluation
When you purchase an Evaluation:

- You pay a one-time fee for the selected account size and type.
- The account size and type cannot be changed after purchase.
- The fee is non-refundable and cannot be transferred.
- The purchase provides access to trade the Evaluation for 30 calendar days.


30-Day Access Period
Each Evaluation includes 30 consecutive calendar days of access.

Important details:

- The 30 days run continuously from the moment of purchase.
- Calendar days include weekends and holidays.
- The access period is not based on trading days.

The Evaluation expires automatically at 11:59 PM ET on Day 30.

Once the 30-day period ends:

- The account expires automatically.
- There are no recurring charges.
- There is nothing to cancel.
- The account cannot be extended.


Expiration Before Reaching Profit Target
If the Evaluation expires before the profit target is reached:

- The Evaluation closes.
- The current balance does not carry over.
- The account cannot be extended or continued.

To keep trading, you must purchase a new Evaluation, which starts a new 30-day access period with a new starting balance.


Maximum Drawdown Failure
If the Evaluation reaches its maximum drawdown at any time during the 30-day period:

- The Evaluation immediately fails.
- The account is permanently closed.
- There are no reset options.

To try again, you must purchase a new Evaluation.


No Cancellation Required
Unlike Legacy accounts, the new Evaluation plans are not subscription-based.

Because they are not subscriptions:

- There are no recurring charges.
- There is nothing to cancel.
- The Evaluation simply expires at the end of the 30-day access period.

You can verify the expiration date of Intraday and EOD Evaluations in the Active Evaluations section of your dashboard.


Passing the Evaluation
If you reach the profit target and meet all Evaluation rules within the 30-day access period:

- The account is reviewed at market close (4:59:59 PM ET).
- If approved, the Evaluation is marked as Passed after 6 PM ET.

Once your Evaluation is marked as Passed:

- You have 7 calendar days to activate your Performance Account (PA).
- Activation requires completing the PA Activation Fee.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Evaluation a subscription?
No. An Evaluation is a one-time payment that provides 30 days of access. There are no monthly charges and nothing renews automatically.

Do I need to cancel my Evaluation?
No cancellation is required. The Evaluation automatically expires after 30 calendar days and you will not be charged again.

Do I get 30 trading days?
No. The access period is 30 consecutive calendar days starting from the moment of purchase. Because this includes weekends and holidays, the number of actual trading sessions may vary.

Can I extend my Evaluation if I run out of time?
No. The 30-day access period cannot be extended. To continue trading, you must purchase a new Evaluation which begins a new 30-day period with a new starting balance.

What happens to my profit if my Evaluation expires?
If the Evaluation expires before reaching the profit target, the account closes and the balance does not transfer. A new Evaluation starts with a fresh starting balance.

Can I change the account size or type after purchase?
No. The account size and type chosen at checkout cannot be modified later. To trade a different account size or type, you must purchase a new Evaluation.

What happens if I reach the maximum drawdown?
If the maximum drawdown is reached during the 30-day period, the Evaluation fails and the account is closed. To try again, you must purchase a new Evaluation.

Are there reset fees?
No. There are no reset fees. If an Evaluation fails, the only way to continue is by purchasing a new Evaluation.

When does the 7-day PA activation window begin?
The 7-day activation window begins when the Evaluation is officially marked as Passed after market close (after 6 PM ET), not when the profit target is reached during the trading day.

What if I reach my profit target on the final day?
If the target profit is reached on the final day of the Evaluation, it still counts.

As long as the profit target is reached before market close on the expiration date, the Evaluation will be marked as Passed after the end-of-day reports are processed (before midnight on the expiration date).

Once marked as Passed, you will have 7 full calendar days to activate your Performance Account.

What happens if I do not activate my PA within 7 days?
If the PA activation fee is not completed within 7 calendar days, the activation option expires and cannot be reopened. To qualify again, you must pass a new Evaluation.

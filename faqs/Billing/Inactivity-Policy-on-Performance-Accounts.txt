Inactivity Policy on Performance Accounts (PA)

Overview
Intraday and End of Day (EOD) Performance Accounts must remain active by meeting a minimum trading activity requirement.

To keep a Performance Account active, you must record at least 2 trading days with $50 or more in net profit within every rolling 30-day period.

This requirement is measured continuously.


Activity Requirement
For Intraday and EOD Performance Accounts to remain active:

- At least 2 trading days must show $50 or more in net profit.
- These qualifying profit days must occur within every rolling 30-day window.
- This rule is evaluated continuously based on the most recent 30 calendar days.


Dormant Phase
If an account does not meet the required activity threshold:

- After 15 days of inactivity, the account enters the dormant phase.
- Dormant accounts appear in the "Dormant PAs" section of your user dashboard.

During the dormant phase:

- Daily notifications are sent reminding you that the account is inactive.
- The notifications warn that the account may be permanently closed if activity requirements are not met.


Account Closure Due to Inactivity
If 30 consecutive calendar days pass without meeting the required activity level:

- The Performance Account will be closed due to inactivity.

When a Performance Account is closed due to inactivity:

- Access to the account is permanently removed.
- Any accumulated rewards or payout eligibility are forfeited.
- Closed accounts cannot be reinstated.


Purpose of the Policy
This rule exists to ensure active participation and consistent engagement with Performance Accounts.


Frequently Asked Questions

What does “rolling 30 days” mean?
A rolling 30-day window means the system continuously looks back at the most recent 30 consecutive calendar days.

If there are fewer than 2 qualifying profit days within that period, the account becomes eligible for inactivity closure.


Does placing trades without reaching $50 profit prevent closure?
No. Simply placing trades does not prevent inactivity closure.

The account must record at least 2 trading days with $50 or more in net profit within the rolling 30-day period.


Does a breakeven trading day count?
No. A trading day must close with at least $50 in profit in order to qualify as an activity day.


Can inactivity closure be reversed?
No. Once a Performance Account is closed due to inactivity, it cannot be reinstated.


Does this policy apply to Legacy accounts?
No. Legacy Performance Accounts follow a different inactivity policy.

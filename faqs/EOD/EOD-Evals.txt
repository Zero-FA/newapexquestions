EOD Evaluations

Overview
The End-of-Day (EOD) Evaluation is a 30-day assessment designed to measure profitability and risk management using an End-of-Day drawdown model.

Key characteristics:

- No intraday trailing drawdown
- End-of-Day (EOD) Drawdown calculated once per day at market close
- EOD drawdown enforced during the following trading session
- Daily Loss Limit (DLL) is fixed during each trading session
- Fixed position size during the evaluation
- No minimum number of trading days required
- The evaluation can be passed in a single trading day
- 7 calendar days are provided to activate the Performance Account (PA) after passing


How EOD Drawdown Works
The EOD Drawdown is calculated once per trading day at market close based on the account’s End-of-Day balance.

Once calculated, the EOD Threshold becomes active during the next trading session.

If the account balance touches or falls below the EOD Threshold at any time:

- All open positions are automatically liquidated
- The evaluation immediately fails

Passing the Evaluation
To successfully pass an EOD Evaluation:

- Reach the required profit target
- Do not touch or breach the EOD Drawdown threshold
- Follow all Apex Trader Funding trading rules

There is no minimum number of trading days required. You may pass the evaluation as soon as the profit target is reached as long as all rules are respected.

Once the evaluation is passed, you will have 7 calendar days to activate the corresponding Performance Account (PA).


Trading Rules

Rule 1 — Do Not Breach the EOD Drawdown
The End-of-Day Threshold defines the lowest allowable account balance.

Key rules:

- The threshold is calculated once per day at market close.
- The threshold becomes active during the next trading session.
- The account may experience temporary intraday drawdown.
- The account may never touch or cross the EOD Threshold.

If the account balance touches or falls below the threshold:

- All positions are liquidated
- The evaluation immediately fails
- Trading stops

To continue trading after a failure, a new evaluation must be purchased.


Rule 2 — No Prohibited Trading Activity
Evaluation accounts may not be passed by hedging positions across multiple accounts.

Accounts must be traded independently using directional trading strategies.

Any prohibited trading behavior or rule circumvention may result in immediate account closure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pass the evaluation in one day?
Yes. There is no minimum trading day requirement. You may pass as soon as the profit target is reached and all rules are respected.


Is there a time limit to reach the profit target?
Yes. The evaluation must be completed within 30 calendar days from the date of account purchase.


Does hitting the Daily Loss Limit fail the evaluation?
No. Reaching the Daily Loss Limit only pauses trading for the remainder of the trading session. The account remains active.


What happens if the EOD Threshold is touched?
If the EOD Threshold is touched or crossed at any time, the evaluation fails immediately.


When does the trading day reset?
The trading day resets at 6:00 PM Eastern Time.


Does the Daily Loss Limit affect the EOD Threshold?
No. The Daily Loss Limit (DLL) and the EOD Threshold are separate rules.


Does the 30-day access period apply to the Performance Account?
No. The 30-day access period applies only to Evaluation accounts.

Intraday Trailing Drawdown Explained

Overview
The Intraday Trailing Drawdown defines the maximum total drawdown allowed on an account.

It adjusts in real time based on the highest account balance reached (Peak Balance). The Peak Balance includes both realized and unrealized gains.

The Intraday Trailing Threshold only moves upward and never moves downward.

If the threshold is breached, all open positions are automatically liquidated. In an Evaluation the account fails immediately, and in a Performance Account the account closes immediately.


Intraday Trailing Drawdown at a Glance

Key characteristics:

- Defines the maximum allowed total drawdown
- Adjusts in real time based on the account’s Peak Balance
- Peak Balance includes realized and unrealized gains
- The trailing threshold only moves upward
- The threshold never moves downward
- If breached, all positions are liquidated automatically
- In Performance Accounts, trailing stops once Starting Balance + $100 is reached


What Is the Intraday Trailing Drawdown?

The Intraday Trailing Drawdown sets the lowest balance the account may reach at any moment during the trading session.

This level is called the Intraday Trailing Threshold.

The threshold follows the highest intraday balance reached (Peak Balance). Each time a new peak balance occurs, the threshold moves upward while maintaining a fixed distance behind the peak based on account size.

Once the threshold moves up, it never moves downward even if the account balance later declines.

The threshold is enforced in real time and includes unrealized profit and loss.


What Happens If the Intraday Threshold Is Breached

If the account balance touches or falls below the Intraday Trailing Threshold at any time:

- All open positions are automatically liquidated

For Evaluations:
- The evaluation fails immediately

For Performance Accounts:
- The account closes immediately


Why the Intraday Trailing Drawdown Exists

The Intraday Trailing Drawdown protects the account from excessive losses while locking in progress as profits increase.

As the account balance rises, the risk protection becomes tighter automatically. This structure encourages disciplined and controlled trading.


How the Intraday Trailing Threshold Is Calculated

The threshold updates continuously throughout the trading session and follows the Peak Balance.

The Peak Balance includes both realized and unrealized gains.

Trailing distance based on account size:

Account Size: 25K  
Max Intraday Drawdown: $1,000

Account Size: 50K  
Max Intraday Drawdown: $2,000

Account Size: 100K  
Max Intraday Drawdown: $3,000

Account Size: 150K  
Max Intraday Drawdown: $4,000


When Intraday Trailing Stops

Performance Accounts
In Performance Accounts, trailing stops once the Intraday Threshold reaches Starting Balance + $100.

Example for a 50K Performance Account:

Threshold Stop Level: $50,100

This occurs when the highest account balance reaches $52,100 (Starting Balance + Max Drawdown + $100).

Once this level is reached, the Intraday Threshold remains fixed at $50,100.


Evaluations — Rithmic and WealthCharts
For Rithmic and WealthCharts Evaluations, the Intraday Threshold stops trailing when it reaches the Target Profit balance.

Example for a 50K Evaluation:

Profit Target Balance: $53,000

Trailing stops when balance reaches: $55,000  
(Profit Target Balance + Max Drawdown)

Final Threshold Stop Level: $53,000


Evaluations — Tradovate
For Tradovate Evaluations, the Intraday Trailing Drawdown does not stop trailing.

The threshold continues to trail indefinitely with the Peak Balance.


Frequently Asked Questions

Do I fail if I reach the Intraday Trailing Threshold?
Yes. If the account balance touches or falls below the threshold at any time:

- All open positions are automatically liquidated

For Evaluations:
- The evaluation fails immediately

For Performance Accounts:
- The account closes immediately


Does unrealized profit move the Trailing Threshold?
Yes. Peak Balance includes both realized and unrealized gains.

If an open position pushes the account to a new high balance, the threshold will move upward immediately even if the position has not been closed.


Does the Trailing Threshold ever move down?
No. Once the threshold moves upward due to a new Peak Balance, it never moves downward.


Does the Intraday Trailing Drawdown reset each day?
No. The trailing threshold follows the highest balance achieved and does not reset daily.


What happens if liquidation fills slightly below the threshold?
Liquidations occur at market price.

Depending on price movement and liquidity, the final balance may appear slightly above or slightly below the threshold.

However, once the threshold is touched, the account is considered breached.


When does trailing stop in a Performance Account?
Trailing stops once the threshold reaches Starting Balance + $100.

Example:
For a $50,000 account the threshold stop level becomes $50,100.


When does trailing stop in Rithmic or WealthCharts Evaluations?
Trailing stops once the threshold reaches the Profit Target Balance.

Example for a $50,000 account:

Profit Target Balance: $53,000  
Trailing locks when balance reaches: $55,000  
Final Threshold Level: $53,000


Does trailing stop in Tradovate Evaluations?
No. Tradovate Evaluations continue trailing indefinitely and do not lock at a fixed level.


Can my account dip below the threshold and recover?
No. If the account touches or drops below the threshold at any moment, liquidation occurs immediately and the account fails or closes.


Is the Intraday Trailing Drawdown the same as a Daily Loss Limit?
No.

Intraday Trailing Drawdown:
- Follows the highest balance achieved
- Enforces a maximum total drawdown

Daily Loss Limit:
- Resets each trading session
- Limits losses within a single trading day

These are separate risk control mechanisms.


Why does the Intraday Trailing Drawdown exist?
It protects the account from excessive losses while automatically strengthening risk protection as profits grow.

As the account balance increases, the allowable drawdown tightens, encouraging consistent and disciplined trading.

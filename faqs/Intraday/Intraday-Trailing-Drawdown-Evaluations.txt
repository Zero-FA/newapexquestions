Intraday Trailing Drawdown Evaluations

Overview
The Intraday Trailing Drawdown Evaluation is a 30-day assessment designed to measure profitability and risk management using a real-time intraday trailing drawdown model.

The Intraday Trailing Threshold moves dynamically based on the highest account balance reached (Peak Balance), including unrealized gains. It is enforced continuously during the trading session.


Intraday Evaluation at a Glance

Key characteristics of the Intraday Evaluation:

- Real-time intraday trailing drawdown follows the Peak Balance
- The trailing threshold never moves downward
- No Daily Loss Limit (DLL)
- Fixed position size during the evaluation
- No minimum trading days required
- The evaluation can be passed in one trading day
- Traders have 7 calendar days to activate a Performance Account after passing

Passing the Evaluation

To pass the Intraday Evaluation, traders must:

- Reach the profit target
- Avoid breaching the Intraday Trailing Drawdown

There is no minimum trading day requirement. The evaluation can be passed as soon as the profit target is reached and all rules are respected.

Once the evaluation is passed, the trader has 7 calendar days to activate the corresponding Intraday Drawdown Performance Account (PA).


Trading Rules

Rule 1 — Do Not Breach the Intraday Trailing Drawdown
The Intraday Trailing Threshold defines the lowest allowed account balance at any time during the trading session.

The threshold moves upward in real time based on the highest balance achieved (Peak Balance), including unrealized gains. It never moves downward.

If the account balance touches or falls below the trailing threshold:

- All open positions are automatically liquidated
- The evaluation is immediately failed

Because liquidation occurs at market price, the final balance after liquidation may appear slightly above or below the threshold depending on market conditions.

Once the threshold is breached, the evaluation is considered failed regardless of the final fill price.


Rule 2 — No Prohibited Trading Activity
Evaluations must be traded using independent directional trading strategies.

Hedging accounts against each other or attempting to circumvent the rules is prohibited.

Any violation of Apex Trader Funding trading policies may result in immediate account closure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pass the evaluation in one day?
Yes. There is no minimum trading day requirement. As soon as the profit target is reached and all rules are followed, the evaluation may be passed.


Is there a Daily Loss Limit?
No. Intraday Trailing Evaluations do not have a Daily Loss Limit.

Risk control is enforced entirely through the real-time trailing threshold.


How does the Trailing Threshold work?
The trailing threshold follows the highest account balance reached (Peak Balance), including unrealized gains.

As the balance increases, the threshold moves upward.

The threshold never moves downward.


What happens if my account closes slightly below the trailing threshold?
Liquidations occur at market price, so the final balance may appear slightly above or below the threshold.

However, once the threshold is touched, the evaluation is considered failed.


Does the trailing threshold reset each day?
No. The trailing threshold continues to follow the highest balance achieved during the evaluation and does not reset daily.


What happens if I pass on the last day of the 30-day access period?
If the profit target is reached within the 30-day access period and all rules are followed, the evaluation will be marked as passed after market close.

You will then have 7 calendar days to activate your Performance Account.


Does the trailing threshold move downward on losing days?
No. The trailing threshold only moves upward with increases in peak balance.


Can I extend the 30-day access period?
No. The Intraday Evaluation provides exactly 30 calendar days of access and extensions are not available.


Does position size increase as profits grow?
No. Contract limits remain fixed during the evaluation and do not scale with profits.


Why is there no consistency rule during the evaluation?
The evaluation phase focuses on profitability under the trailing drawdown model.

Consistency requirements are introduced later during the Performance Account stage.
